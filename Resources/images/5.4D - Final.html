This is a great method for quick organizing data, especially quantitative data.

To create a good affinity diagram, you must follow 7 steps: prepare a workspace, create a team, create cards, sorting cards, label the group, regroup, and walk through space. You also know some strengths and weaknesses of the affinity diagram

Then you can see how the affinity diagram be used in the UCD project to find the requirements to make a good product for users. Introduction

To make a good product for users to use, collecting data is one of the most important things that you have to pay attention to. Because only if you collect data and gather them, you can understand the user's demand for the product. For example, this semester, I have a project named "EnergyShifter". This semester, I have to create an application that helps my user understands energy and gives them advice that which types of energy should be used and when to use them to minimize the electricity costs and optimize energy usage. I use some models to represent my idea in the project such as a workflow model, persona, and hierarchical task inventory but none of them are good for collecting data. Therefore, to collect data effectively, there is a method that is used commonly by many people named affinity diagram. An affinity diagram is a process for clustering and synthesizing a large amount of data into manageable themes that emerge from the data itself (Courage, 2005). This is a great method for quick organizing data, especially quantitative data.

In this report, you will understand why should you use the affinity diagram. You also learn how to create a good affinity diagram and understand the strengths and weaknesses of an affinity diagram. Besides, you can see how the affinity diagram be implemented in the UCD project. Finally, you can find some useful recommendations about how to create a good affinity diagram.

Why should you use an affinity diagram?

There are some reasons that you want to use the affinity diagram. First, an affinity diagram is a great method to share the results of your study with your stakeholders as the study progresses. They can base on the affinity diagram to find out the evolving trend. According to Courage (2005), the affinity diagram allows for quick data analysis once the study is complete. Another reason for using an affinity diagram is that with an affinity diagram, you can arrange the data of the large issues or problem into a structure. You can divide the issues into smaller parts or combine some small parts into a bigger category. By using an affinity diagram, you will realize which problems are affected by many areas and you also know that where you miss the information. Therefore, you can solve the problem easier. When you use an affinity diagram, if you want to present your idea or solution, you can point directly to the user data on the diagram that informs your opinion as evidence. Affinity diagram can be innovative because you do not work with any preconceived categories. You can find some new ideas that emerged from the data.

Creating an affinity diagram

If you want to create an affinity diagram, there are some steps which will be mentioned below that you have to follow.

Step 1: Prepare a workspace.

An affinity diagram will be created on a board or a wall in your workspace, laboratory, or conference room. The more information collected for the affinity diagram, the bigger the board's size is needed. Because of that, a workspace that you prepare needs to have enough space for a team to work effectively because you will create an affinity diagram with a team mostly. One thing that you have to concern about is you must ensure that the board or the wall that is used to create an affinity diagram must be secured if you do not want your diagram to be cleaned by co-workers or cleaning staff.

Step 2: Create a team

According to Courage (2005), the affinity diagram works best as a team approach. Therefore, you should find your team members to create a team and add them to a specific role to do such as moderator. Ensuring that everybody will contribute to the work and involve anybody related to work together. The more people you have, the faster the speed of analyzing data. The point of view of each member is essential because from people's opinions, new ideas will be found and it can be used to solve the problems. Creativity needs to be encouraged and the criticism of people's ideas or opinions will not be allowed.

Step 3: Create the cards:

Each member should have some cards to write their opinions, questions, quotes, or anything related to the topic and pin them to the board or the wall. Choosing different colors for each type of data or each data collected by a team member is necessary to distinguish the type of data that the team has or data collected by each person. Depending on the number of participants or the length of the data collected, each person will need to prepare at least 50 cards or above for any problems.

Step 4: Sorting cards:

Once you and your team gather all cards, each person will receive some cards and start working on them. If you think that a card should be duplicated to put into another cluster, you should do it. If any problems happened in multiple groups, you can duplicate the issue into different notes or cards to help people solving the problem. When you group some similar cards, it is not necessary to explain why you put them together because it can be the gut feeling. Only label the categories if you finish grouping all information that is related together. If you find some similar problems or issues, put them on top of each other so that you can tell your team that there are some indicate issues or problems.

Step 5: Label the group:

After gathering all related information, you should label the group as a title or a short description to summarize the information and distinguish it from other groups.

Step 6: Regroup:

When you sort data, you need to have a look at the duplicate groups to eliminate them. You should look for a small and large group because it is better if you add more information to the small group to make it meaningful or divide the large group into some sub-group if necessary.

Step 7: Walk through the diagram

After arranging the group and label them, you and your team should have a look through the affinity diagram. You can write some notes or record the discussion because it will be helpful to find the result of the study. The team should agree with each description of each group. If anybody wants to add more cards to the diagram, just add them.

While creating the affinity diagram, taking a picture of the final affinity diagram will help you to demonstrate to the stakeholders that how your data be grouped. People who are unfamiliar with the affinity diagram can use the picture to understand what the affinity diagram shows them. Although they cannot read the whole data on the picture at least they can understand what your team does.

Strengths and weaknesses of an affinity diagram

Strength

Affinity diagram brings many advantages for people using that. First, an affinity diagram is a simple method for people to collect data from users. Everything you need to create an affinity diagram is notes, a pen, and aboard. You do not need to pay for any modern and expensive equipment to make a good affinity diagram. You can create an affinity diagram by working individually but it is ideal to work as a team. Your teammates can find new ideas that emerged from the affinity diagram. By using an affinity diagram, many people can combine their ideas on their notes, cluster them, and pin them on the board so that anybody can see. Because the affinity diagram contains the ideas of many people, they can work together to generate some useful discussions to solve the problems or develop some ideas. One advantage of the affinity diagram is that you can see the big picture and find out what is the biggest problem. By realizing the biggest problem, people will build a plan to focus on that problem and try to solve it. Another advantage of affinity diagram is that you can base on the affinity diagram to consensus the most important issue with people in your team before work on that issue. With affinity diagram, you can represent your idea with other people and they also understand your idea easily.

Weakness

However, affinity diagram still has some weaknesses. First, if you have lots of data, you need to have good facilitation to help you keep focusing on your data to work effectively. The next problem of affinity diagram is that affinity diagram is not portable. You cannot bring the board anywhere you want. There are many notes of people on that board and if you lose the board or any notes on the board, you will have to create it again. It is time-consuming and you never want to spend time re-creating another affinity diagram. Another problem is that if you have lots of data, you have to spend much time working with your data to create an affinity diagram and it can be time-consuming. The small size of the notes is a problem. It forces people to write briefly and sometimes it is hard for them to write a meaningful idea only on small notes. With lots of data on the affinity diagram, it is hard for people to find the individual pieces of data. The last problem is that the affinity diagram is only temporary. If you want to save the diagram, you must take a photo of it for a permanent record.








Affinity diagram for the UCD Project

Based on the interview data of the project, I create an affinity diagram for the UCD project which is shown below.














Theme generated from affinity diagram

There are 4 themes that are shown on the affinity diagram above. Each theme is some different ways that the participant uses to reduce the electricity costs.

Energy Tariff

To save money, the participants decide to use appliances when off-peak time They also set a delay before turning the appliances on.

Optimize the solar energy

To optimize the amount of energy which is generated, the participants decide to use high energy appliances on a sunny day and turn off the devices after sundown. They also check the weather forecast carefully to build a plan for using appliances.

Reduce energy use

The first theme is reducing energy use. To achieve the target, the participants try to use heating and cooling appliances only if necessary.

Track energy use

The second theme shows some methods that the participants use to track energy use. They use a website to monitor the energy usage, check the gadget to know the amount of energy that is used and generated.

Requirements of an affinity diagram

Reg-18: Tracking energy use.

Requirement: The user shall be able to track how much energy they use[B]. Rationale: Users always want to know how much energy they use so that highlighting the amount of energy use is appropriate.

Reg-19: Weather situation

Requirement: The user shall be able to know the weather situation[D]. Rationale: Users always update the weather situation. They will base on the information to decide what to do each day.

Reg-20: Off-peak time

Requirement: The user shall be able to know what they should do at the off-peak time[C] Rationale: Giving users some advice about what they should do at the off-peak time helps them save money.

Reg-21: Reduce energy use

Requirement: The user shall be able to know which appliance uses high energy[A]. Rationale: If users know which device that uses high energy, they can decrease the use of them.

Recommendation

After doing the affinity diagram for the project, I realize that this diagram is helpful. When you look at the diagram, you will see a big picture of the issue. This helps you to understand the main issues and also find the problems to improve the quality of the diagram and solve problems. If students have to create an affinity diagram for the next UCD project, I highly recommend them to do that. Students should try to use the affinity diagram even if not asked to do it formally in the project because it is easy to learn how to do an affinity diagram and affinity diagrams bring many benefits for people who use that.

Conclusion

In conclusion, an affinity diagram is a good method for people to collect and cluster data into manageable groups. With affinity diagram, you will have a big picture of the problem. You can base on the diagram to analyze data to write some requirements. After the study, you will understand what affinity diagram is, how to create an affinity diagram, strengths and weaknesses and how the affinity diagram is applied to the UCD project.
